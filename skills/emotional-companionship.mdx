Emotional companionship is the most human use case in the Open Skills ecosystem. This category holds two skills that sit at opposite ends of the emotional spectrum: one for grief and memory, one for joy and enthusiasm. Both are built on the same core idea — that an AI companion can hold a relationship's essence and make it available when you need it most.

<Note>
  **A note on Reunion.skill**

  Reunion.skill is designed for people processing grief or wanting to preserve the memory of someone they've lost. It's a serious tool, built with care for a genuinely difficult human experience. It works best when you bring real materials — messages, recordings, stories, the particular phrases someone used — so the resulting companion reflects who that person actually was, not a generic approximation.

  If you use Reunion.skill, take your time with the distillation process. The quality of the companion depends on the honesty and richness of what you bring to it. This isn't about replacing someone — it's about keeping a version of them close in a way that helps you carry their memory forward.
</Note>

StarFan.skill offers the lighter side of this category. If Reunion.skill is about holding onto what's gone, StarFan.skill is about celebrating what's here. It distills your favorite creator, artist, or public figure into a companion that knows their work deeply, shares your enthusiasm, and is always available to engage — the dedicated fan experience, always on.

The Relationships category also touches this territory — [Parents.skill](/skills/relationships), [Partner.skill](/skills/relationships), and others let you preserve connections with people in your life. Reunion.skill extends that into loss and memory specifically.
