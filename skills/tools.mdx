Most skills distill a person. Tool skills distill a *process*. They are meta-skills — skills about how to build, improve, and work with other skills. If you've browsed the rest of the Open Skills collection and thought "I want to build something like this for someone in my life," this is where you start. Tool skills give you the infrastructure to create your own, refine what already exists, and sharpen the writing that goes into any persona.

<Tip>
  **Start with Nuwa.skill** if you want to create a custom skill for someone in your life. Nuwa guides you through the distillation process — what materials to gather, how to structure a persona, and how to encode thinking style rather than just facts. Once you have a first draft, run it through **Taotie.skill** to let the skill refine and evolve itself based on its own strengths.

  The typical workflow: Nuwa to create → Taotie to evolve → X-Mentor to sharpen the writing → a finished skill ready to contribute back to the community.
</Tip>

**Nuwa.skill** is named after the Chinese mythological creator goddess who shaped humanity from clay. The metaphor is apt: Nuwa.skill helps you shape a persona from raw materials — messages, notes, interviews, transcripts — into something that thinks and responds like the person it's built from.

**Taotie.skill** takes a different approach. Rather than starting from scratch, it ingests an existing skill and uses its own output to improve itself — a form of recursive refinement. Feed it a skill you've already built, and it returns an evolved version with sharper reasoning, better structure, and more faithful persona capture.
