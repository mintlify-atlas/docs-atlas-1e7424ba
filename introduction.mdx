Open Skills is an open-source collection of community-built AI persona skills. Each `.skill` file encodes the thinking style, communication patterns, and worldview of a real person — a colleague, a legendary investor, a Buddhist master, or a departed loved one — and turns that person into an interactive AI companion you can converse with. The project is bilingual, with skills contributed in both Chinese and English, and the catalog continues to grow through community contributions.

Whether you want to practice a difficult conversation with a boss persona, stress-test an investment thesis against a Buffett-style reasoner, or simply explore how a historical thinker would approach a modern problem, Open Skills gives you a structured way to do it. Every skill in the collection lives in its own GitHub repository, is freely available, and follows a consistent format so you can load it into your preferred AI platform in minutes.

## Bilingual by design

Open Skills is a community that spans Chinese and English speakers. Skill repositories may include system prompts, README files, and sample interactions in either or both languages. When you browse the catalog, look for the language notes in each skill's repository to understand which languages are supported.

<Note>
  Open Skills is fully open-source under the MIT license. Every skill in the collection is a community contribution, and new skills are welcome. If you have a persona you want to distill, see the [contributing guide](/contributing/how-to-contribute) to learn how to publish your own skill.
</Note>
